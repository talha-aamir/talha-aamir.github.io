SDE: Software Development Extremism

Or, as I like to call it, Hot Take Bonanza

One thing that truly fascinates me over my time of reading and watching videos by many folks is the extremely 2 sided camps of software. There is the middle ground of everyday webdev normies, but that's normal and the uninteresting bit.
You see, these two camps, from what I have seen are:

  • Linux/FOSS Purists
  • Game-dev, Performant software purists
Both camps have small similarity, and also huge differences. I guess what I'm trying to say is that it's rather interesting to see this, abit comical as well that a situaition like this exists. I should mention that for the majority of the points I agree with both camps.
Generalising and combining a group of people who are passionate about good quality software is also bad, because that is the point of both sides. To have good quality, bloat less, and high performance software. It's also a fair point; alot of people have no idea how fast their computers are, or their phones even, and to be honest have been spoiled by not only the web but just bad software and unoptimized code to make them think that a better pc will fix all their issues. While this isn't true for a majority of the games, because that is one industry still producing competent software that runs blazingly fast, this is the case for just about all things web. We're getting there with the web, too, but that's another thing, and I've steered away from the point.
On with the camps

The Stallman FOSS Bloat-less Enthusiast Foundation

So, this is the most interesting, actual rabbit hole of bloat-less, spyware-less, open source, free software. You see, initially, it was Richard Stallman who introduced the notion of open source software, software that is not propietary and the users of which know and can see all that the softwares they use are doing. He arguably had the greatest impact on our software world, which still permeates all open source git repositories to this day. Now I haven't researched the start of the open source movement, so I can't say if he was alone in this, I'm sure he wasn't, but you can do that search on your own. Anyways... so the linux foss enthusiast community has a motto or a set of conditions for software they consider good, useful and meaningful.

  • FOSS: Free Open Source Software
  • Follows Unix Philosophy
Now, you know FOSS, the title is self-explanatory, but I'll explain the unix philosophy.

The Unix Philosophy

The unix philosophy states, that software should be simple, it should do one thing, and it should do that one thing really well. That is it.

Now, what's interesting is how far this community is willing to go with that philosophy. So, firstly, following FOSS means,
you stick to only linux, I agree with that, windows sucks, and I hate apple and microsoft, as any sane man should. Think more about this though, and you'll realise, this also means that you stick to open source browsers (which is easy), you don't use websites like facebook, twitter, instagram, or any other bloatware (that's also easy). Well, if/when you use this, then you make sure to have somewhat strong anti-tracking tools/browser settings, or use these websites with FOSS front-ends, that will atleast protect you from the front-end tracking they do (and they do that alot) and to some extent cookies.
Allow me to emphasise though, only foss means Only FOSS, and this also includes your email client, any communication platform you use, any cloud hosting, any storage solution, any piece of code you run, has to be FOSS. Why?, well the argument is actually pretty sensible, you can't tell what proprietary software is doing, and it can and sure as hell, does and will track you. It would be stupid of companies to not spy on you and profit off of any data you have. And you're an even bigger chicken for thinking that they won't do it, that's just naive and foolish.
Here's where it gets fun though. This extends to even the kernal, and any piece of code your laptop runs. A big gripe with alot of people is the spyware of the intel management engine, and a similar tool for amd. These are piece of code that your motherboard runs regardless, which gives this companies, complete and total control of your entire system. It's described, rightfully so, as a backdoor into your entire machine. But....I'm sure they won't do anything harmful, I mean why would they, they just make processors. What could they or any company they are affiliated with or are in cahoots with, possibly do from complete control of our system.
Don't fret, we've got something more fun. The unix philosophy. If you think the FOSS philosophy was tough, buddy I have news for you. The extent people are willing to go to use code that isn't bloat, is for anyone outside looking in, quite literally insane. Although they'd argue we are the one's insane here. Take for example, a big gripe people have with linux is how much bloat alot of the software is. Most common tools, Sudo, and SystemD are bloat. They argue, whilst still making sense, that firstly, sudo as its used is bloat for everyday users, it does many things for sys administration, we need something alot simpler. But, thats a minor point, a bigger thing is this thing called systemD, which people state, is bloat software that tries to manage your entire system, and has been the bane of my youtube recommendation system with that one guy's video getting recommended everyday titled, the tragedy of system D. You see systemD basically is a suite of daemons, that manage your core system functionality from boot, to ensure that all your devices, drivers, clocks, any settings you can expect from a normal, functional system, work automatically without hassle. Now purists argue, it does too much for a system that is supposed to just init your system, and are willing to switch their os to one that does not use systemD. As I said, this is all bizarre yet interesting.

Performance Extremists

I wouldn't even call this group extremists, but these are people who write good, performant and functional software, or have experience with writing it, and are fed up with the current industry "standards" or "practices". They're actually more sensible and make points the mainstream dev, might agree with. Then again, I might be expecting too much from the mainstream dev. Their requirement is,

  • Software should be performant
  • Code should be written as simply as one can, without any convoluted practices that pollute it.
Both of these are self-explanatory, what I found funny is that, extremists in this end do despise windows, but consider it superior to the buggy mess (not true), that is linux, and consider open source software to be trash. They consider that the standards of code acceptance and just software that comes out of open source work is also pretty bad. I think it's a generalisation and an oversight, but this is where the two sides agree, most software is trash, that is true, but you have to go into the rabbit hole to find actually good software. Lastly, they don't care much about proprietary software, they are just programmers who care about programming, thats it.
